Why it matters?

Sponsorship is a critical success factor in the advancement of diverse talent. Research shows that employees of color and women are less likely to be sponsored in organizations.

This matters as people with sponsors are 23% more likely to advance their careers than those without.

Sponsors leverage their influence, position, and social capital to advocate career advancement for the person they are sponsoring. Leaders also benefit as sponsors are 53% more likely to advance in their careers than those who do not.

What you can do?

Take The Multiplier Effect Pledge to sponsor diverse talent — make a commitment to sponsor at least one employee within your organization that's different from you — seek out talent across the full spectrum of diversity.

Additional Ideas

  • Challenge your peers to take the pledge
  • Use #MultiplyDiversity — be a champion of change and an advocate for a sponsorship culture
